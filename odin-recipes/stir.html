Stir Fry Recipe

Description:

Enjoy the delightful simplicity of my Vegetable Stir Fry, a quick and satisfying one-pan dish that can be ready in under 30 minutes. Sauteed to perfection, an array of vibrant and crisp veggies harmoniously blend with an easy-to-make sweet and savory stir fry sauce. This recipe is your go-to solution when the craving for Asian takeout strikes, providing a homemade alternative that guarantees a burst of authentic flavors and wholesome goodness in every bite.

Ingredients

  • - 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • - 1/2 cup carrots peeled and sliced
  • - 1 cup mushrooms
  • - 1/2 tsp cumin
  • - 1 cup broccoli florets
  • - 3/4 cup bell peppers sliced, red & yellow
  • - 1 cup snow peas trimmed
  • - 1/2 cup water chestnuts drained and sliced
  • - 3/4 cup baby corn drained and sliced
  • - 2 teaspoons garlic minced
  • - 1 teaspoon ginger minced
  • - 1 teaspoon salt and pepper to taste
  • - 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • - 3 tablespoons honey
  • - 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • - 1/4 cup vegetable broth

Steps

1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium high heat. Add the carrots and mushrooms and cook for 4-6 minutes or until tender.

2. Add the broccoli, bell peppers, snow peas, water chestnuts, baby corn and 2 tablespoons of water to the pan.

3. Cook for an additional 3-5 minutes or until vegetables are tender and water has mostly cooked off.

4. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 30 seconds. Season the vegetables with salt and pepper to taste.

5. In a bowl, whisk together the vegetable broth, soy sauce and honey.

2. Pour the soy sauce mixture over the vegetables and cook for 30 seconds.

3. In a small bowl mix the cornstarch with a tablespoon of cold water.

4. Add the cornstarch and bring to a boil; cook for 1 more minute or until sauce has just started to thicken.

5. Serve immediately, over noodles.
