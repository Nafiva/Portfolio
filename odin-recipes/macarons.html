Macarons Recipe

Description:

Immerse yourself in the enchanting world of macarons, delicate and intricate French confections that embody elegance and delight. These dainty treats, with their smooth, glossy shells and enticing fillings, are a testament to the artistry and craftsmanship that goes into their creation. Gently bite into the delicate shell, and you'll discover a moment of pure bliss as it gives way to a tender, chewy center.

Ingredients

MACARONS

  • - 1 ¾ cups powdered sugar
  • - 1 cup almond flour, finely ground
  • - 1 teaspoon salt, divided
  • - 3 egg whites, at room temperature
  • - ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • - ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • - 2 drops pink gel food coloring

VANILLA BUTTERCREAM

  • - 1 cup unsalted butter, 2 sticks, at room temperature
  • - 3 cups powdered sugar
  • - 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • - 3 tablespoons heavy cream

Steps

1. Make the macarons: In the bowl of a food processor, combine the powdered sugar, almond flour, and ½ teaspoon of salt, and process on low speed, until extra fine. Sift the almond flour mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a large bowl.

2. In a separate large bowl, beat the egg whites and the remaining ½ teaspoon of salt with an electric hand mixer until soft peaks form. Gradually add the granulated sugar until fully incorporated. Continue to beat until stiff peaks form.

3. Add the vanilla and beat until incorporated. Add the food coloring and beat until just combined.

4. Add about ⅓ of the sifted almond flour mixture at a time to the beaten egg whites and use a spatula to gently fold until combined. After the last addition of almond flour, continue to fold slowly until the batter falls into ribbons and you can make a figure 8 while holding the spatula up..

5. Transfer the macaron batter into a piping bag fitted with a round tip.

6. Place 4 dots of the batter in each corner of a rimmed baking sheet, and place a piece of parchment paper over it,

7. Let the macarons sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to 1 hour, until dry to the touch.

8. Preheat the oven to 300˚F (150˚C).

9. Bake the macarons for 17 minutes, until the feet are well-risen and the macarons don’t stick to the parchment paper.

10. Make the buttercream: In a large bowl, add the butter and beat with a mixer for 1 minute until light and fluffy. Sift in the powdered sugar and beat until fully incorporated. Add the vanilla and beat to combine.

11. Place in an airtight container for 24 hours to “bloom”.

12. Enjoy with love!
